# Go-To-Market Launch Plan

## Objective

Launch Kairis as a trust-first trading operations product for beginner and early-intermediate crypto users who want more structure than an exchange app and less exposure than a public bot.

## Launch Sequence

### Phase 1: Founder Validation

- use internal owner mode
- validate signal-to-execution workflows
- refine logs, limits, and halt behavior

### Phase 2: Private Beta

- invite a small group of beginner users
- keep them in paper mode first
- observe onboarding failures and support patterns

### Phase 3: Paid Assisted Launch

- release paid exchange-connected workflows
- position around control and clarity
- keep public unattended automation out of scope

## Initial Audience

- beginner retail crypto users
- users willing to pay for guidance and structure
- users on supported centralized exchanges

## Core Launch Offer

- free paper mode plus signals
- paid assisted execution with stronger controls and reporting

## Acquisition Channels

- founder content
- educational threads and short-form posts
- trading communities
- direct waitlist landing page

## Activation Path

1. user joins waitlist
2. user learns the mode model
3. user enters paper mode
4. user connects exchange safely
5. user upgrades for assisted live execution

## Retention Strategy

- daily or weekly review habit through logs and journaling
- visible control wins such as prevented trades or enforced pauses
- exports that reduce user friction around tracking

## Metrics

- waitlist to signup conversion
- signup to paper activation
- paper to live assisted conversion
- paid retention after first month
- support tickets per activated live user

## Launch Risks

- users may expect automation too early
- trust may be fragile if copy promises too much
- support may spike around exchange setup

## Mitigations

- keep public promise narrow
- provide strong onboarding guidance
- make paper mode the default starting experience
