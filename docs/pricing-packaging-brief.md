# Pricing And Packaging Brief

## Packaging Strategy

Packaging must reinforce control maturity. The entry point should not push beginners directly into live unattended behavior.

## Free Tier

Target user:

- curious or early-stage trader

Includes:

- educational onboarding
- paper trading
- basic signals
- limited journal and history views
- limited analytics

Goal:

- build trust and qualify users for paid conversion

## Pro Tier

Target user:

- active user who wants connected workflows and stronger controls

Includes:

- exchange connections
- manual and assisted live workflows
- advanced controls and limits
- detailed trade logs
- exports and reporting features
- deeper analytics

Goal:

- establish recurring subscription value without needing public unattended automation

## Internal Owner Mode

Target user:

- founder / operator only

Includes:

- full automation capability
- stricter internal flags
- explicit separation from public entitlement logic

Goal:

- validate automation architecture without exposing it as a public default

## Future Premium Automation Tier

Not part of initial launch. Consider only after:

- support burden is manageable
- audit and controls are proven
- policy and legal review are revisited

## Packaging Rules

- automation availability must be policy-gated, not billing-only
- live execution should never be the first experience
- free tier should be useful enough to build habit, not just act as a teaser
